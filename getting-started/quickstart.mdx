---
title: "Quickstart"
description: "Get productive with Open Community Groups in 10–15 minutes."
---

Use this walkthrough to experience the core OCG flow in one sitting. By the end, you will have discovered events and, if you sign in, joined a group, RSVP'd to an event, and seen which dashboards are available to you.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign in (optional)">
    Open the **Log in** page, choose **Linux Foundation SSO**, complete authentication, and return to the home page.

    <Note>
      You can skip sign-in if you only want to browse public pages. Membership actions, RSVP, speaker submissions, and dashboard access all require login.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Explore events and groups">
    From the **Explore** page, start with **Events** to see what is happening soon, then switch to **Groups** to find where you want to participate long term.

    <Tip>
      Start broad, then narrow: filter by community first, then add one or two additional filters (type, region, category).
    </Tip>

    Once you have narrowed by community, choose the view that fits your goal:

    - **Events**: use **List** for scanning and **Calendar** for planning.
    - **Groups**: use **List** for scanning and **Map** for location-based discovery.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Join a group">
    Open a group page and click **Join group**. This makes your account an active member of that group, which unlocks member-level communications and creates a cleaner path to event participation.

    After joining, OCG sends a welcome message with a link back to the group page.

    If you later want to stop following the group, **Leave group** reverses the membership cleanly.
  </Step>

  <Step title="RSVP to an event">
    From the group page or explore results, open an event and click **Attend event**.

    This records your attendance intent and is the basis for event-day check-in. For live virtual or hybrid events that have meeting information configured, attendees can also get **Join meeting** access at the right time window.

    After RSVP, OCG sends a confirmation message with a calendar attachment.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Apply to speak (optional)">
    If an event has a Call for Speakers open, create a proposal in **User Dashboard → Session proposals**, then submit that proposal from the event page.

    After submission, track lifecycle changes in **User Dashboard → Submissions**.

    For speaker-side status detail, see the [User Dashboard Guide](/guides/user-dashboard). For organizer-side CFS and review controls, see [Event Operations](/guides/event-operations).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Validate your dashboard access">
    Open your avatar menu in the top-right corner to confirm which dashboard scopes your account has right now.

    - **User Dashboard** is available to all logged-in users.
    - **Community Dashboard** appears when you have community-team access.
    - **Group Dashboard** appears when you have group-team access.

    <Note>
      This step requires logging in with Linux Foundation SSO. If a dashboard entry is missing, your account does not yet have access to that scope.
    </Note>
  </Step>
</Steps>

Not sure which dashboard to use next? Continue with [Choose your dashboard](/getting-started/choose-dashboard).
