OCG has three dashboards, each supporting a different kind of work: personal tasks (User), community management (Community), and group or event management (Group).

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="User Dashboard" icon="user" href="/guides/user-dashboard">
    Manage your profile, invitations, session proposals, and submission history. Available to every logged-in user.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Community Dashboard" icon="building" href="/guides/community-dashboard">
    Manage community-wide identity, team access, taxonomy, analytics, and groups. Requires community-team membership.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Group Dashboard" icon="users" href="/guides/group-dashboard">
    Run events, organizers, members, sponsors, and day-to-day operations. Requires group-team membership.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Pick the right workspace

Start with what you need to do, not the menu label:

| If you need to... | Use this dashboard | Who gets it | Core outcomes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Manage your profile, invitations, proposals, and submissions | User Dashboard | Any logged-in user | Keep profile current, accept invitations, submit to CFS |
| Manage community-wide identity, admins, taxonomy, analytics, and groups | Community Dashboard | Community team members | Manage team access, branding, regions, categories, analytics, and groups |
| Run events, organizers, members, sponsors, and day-to-day operations | Group Dashboard | Group team members | Deliver events, manage members, coordinate organizers and sponsors |

## Access model

Dashboard visibility depends on your role. If a dashboard is missing from your menu, your account does not currently have access to it.

The two organizer dashboards also depend on selected context:

- **Community Dashboard** requires a selected community.
- **Group Dashboard** requires both a selected community and a selected group.

<Note>
  Dashboard access is based on team membership, not a setting you can toggle. If a dashboard entry is missing, you either do not have access yet or have not selected the right context.   Accept any pending invitations in **User Dashboard → Invitations** and refresh the page.
</Note>

## Fixed role model

OCG uses fixed roles for community and group management. Roles are assigned per team member and define what operations are allowed.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Community roles">
    | Role | What it can do |
    | --- | --- |
    | `admin` | Full community management (settings, taxonomy, team, groups) and group-level write operations in that community |
    | `groups-manager` | Manage groups and group-level write operations in that community, without community settings, taxonomy, or team control |
    | `viewer` | Read-only access |

    Controls are disabled in the UI when your role cannot perform an operation. Server-side authorization still enforces permissions even if a request is sent manually.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Group roles">
    | Role | What it can do |
    | --- | --- |
    | `admin` | Full group management (events, members, settings, sponsors, team) |
    | `events-manager` | Event operations only (events) |
    | `viewer` | Read-only access |

    Controls are disabled in the UI when your role cannot perform an operation. Server-side authorization still enforces permissions even if a request is sent manually.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## How to check your access

Open your avatar menu in the top-right corner. The dashboards listed there are the ones your account can access right now.

If a dashboard you expect is missing, use this order to unblock quickly:

  1. Open **User Dashboard → Invitations**.
  2. Accept any pending community or group invitations.
  3. Refresh the page (or sign out and sign back in).
  4. Re-open your avatar menu and check for new dashboard entries.

If visibility still does not update, see [Troubleshooting](/support/troubleshooting).

## Typical starting paths

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Member or speaker">
    Start in the **User Dashboard**. Complete your profile, then create session proposals only when you need to submit to an event's Call for Speakers.

    Continue with the [User Dashboard Guide](/guides/user-dashboard) and [Public Site Guide](/guides/public-site).
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Community lead">
    Start in **Community Dashboard → Settings** to verify settings and team membership. Then review Regions, Group Categories, and Event Categories. Move to Groups after taxonomy and ownership structure are in place.

    Continue with the [Community Dashboard Guide](/guides/community-dashboard).
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Group organizer">
    Start in **Group Dashboard → Settings** and move straight into Events, because event setup, publishing, attendance, and submissions are the operational center of group work.

    Continue with the [Group Dashboard Guide](/guides/group-dashboard) and [Event Operations](/guides/event-operations).
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
