## Account and login

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Is OCG mobile friendly?">
    Public pages are fully usable on mobile devices. Dashboards — including the user, community, and group dashboards — are currently desktop-only.

    For browsing groups, discovering events, RSVPing, checking in, and submitting to Call for Speakers, mobile is supported via the public site.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Events

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I check in without RSVPing first?">
    No. Only attendees who have RSVP'd can check in. There is no walk-in check-in option — you must have registered for the event with your account beforehand.

    <Note>
      The check-in window opens 2 hours before the event starts and closes at the end of the event day.
    </Note>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I use automatic meeting creation for in-person events?">
    No. Automatic meeting creation is supported only for `virtual` and `hybrid` event types. In-person events do not support this feature.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Are automatically created meetings ended automatically?">
    Yes. Meetings created automatically by OCG are also ended automatically when the configured end time is reached. No manual action is needed to close the meeting.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Why are host controls missing in automatically created Zoom meetings?">
    Host controls are not currently available in automatically created Zoom meetings due to technical limitations. This is a known constraint, not a configuration error.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Groups

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Where can I see platform-wide growth trends?">
    Platform-wide growth and activity trends are available on the public **Stats** page. This page shows aggregated data across all communities, groups, and events on OCG.

    <Tip>
      The Stats page is public and does not require you to be logged in.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Speaker program

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Where do I submit a speaker proposal?">
    Speaker proposals are a two-step process:

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Create your session proposal">
        Go to [User Dashboard → Session proposals](/guides/user-dashboard) and create a new proposal. Fill in the title, abstract, and any other required fields.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Submit to a specific event">
        Navigate to the event's public page and open the Call for Speakers (CFS) modal. Select your proposal from the list and submit.
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    You can submit the same proposal to multiple events. Proposals already submitted to a given event will be disabled in that event's CFS modal.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Why is my proposal missing in the CFS modal?">
    If a proposal you created does not appear when you open the CFS submission modal, the most likely causes are:

    - The proposal is not in an eligible status for submission.
    - The proposal was already submitted to this event — already-submitted proposals are hidden or disabled.
    - You are logged in with a different account than the one that owns the proposal.

    Review your proposals and their statuses in [User Dashboard → Session proposals](/guides/user-dashboard).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I undo a submission?">
    You can withdraw a submission while review is still active. Once a final decision has been made — such as `approved` or `linked` — withdraw is no longer available.

    <Warning>
      Submission withdrawal is not possible after a final outcome is recorded. If you need to update a submission, use the `Resubmit` action when the status is `Information requested`.
    </Warning>

    See the [Troubleshooting](/support/troubleshooting) page for a full breakdown of which actions are available by submission status.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Notifications

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Why is the email send button disabled?">
    The send action for group member or event attendee emails is disabled when the recipient count is zero. This prevents sending to an empty list.

    Also confirm that both required fields are filled:

    - **Title** — the email subject line
    - **Body** — the plain text message content

    If the recipient count appears lower than expected, verify that the group has active members or that the event has confirmed attendees.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
